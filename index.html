Scenario-Based Design Tool

This tool used speculative design methods (Blythe, 2014) and sci-fi prototyping (Bleecker, 2009) to explore interaction design through narrative. The team created persona sheets, story templates, and interaction sketches to test ROSE's role as a home nutritionist.

References: IDEO HCD Toolkit, Design Fiction by Julian Bleecker, Research Through Design Fiction by Blythe

Collaborators: Gouri S Vipin, Anagha Viju, Sumit Kundu, Sakshi CHonker

Light and Dark Scenarios

Light Scenario

Mrs. de Groot wakes up. ROSE gently asks if she wants a light breakfast since her sleep quality was low. She accepts, and ROSE prepares a revised plan and hydration reminder.

Dark Scenario

Mr. de Vries reaches for the fridge. ROSE denies access based on recent dietary trends. A conflict escalates, and Mr. de Vries disables ROSE in frustration.

Reflections

Sci-fi Prototyping

Sci-fi scenarios revealed ethical tensions (Bleecker, 2009). Through fiction, we tested boundary conditions in human-robot trust and autonomy.

21st Century Robot

Inspired by Johnson's project, we gave ROSE soft personality traits. It helped us rethink technical function as social behavior.

Rosy Consumer Stories

The "light" scenario creates a trust narrative, but may conceal risks. Design fiction allowed us to contrast it with a darker mirror.

Storytelling in HRI

Storytelling enables designers to test emotional, social, and temporal interaction. Without it, HRI would lack depth and human relatability.

Evidence-Based vs Robot Perception

Users trust robots based on perceived competence. ROSE looked credible through tone and behavior, despite no clinical testing—an HRI design challenge.
