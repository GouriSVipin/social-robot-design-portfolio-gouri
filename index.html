Case Description: ROSE the Care Robot

ROSE, developed by Heemskerk Innovative Technology, is a care robot used in elderly care homes. It supports both nursing and nutrition-related tasks: monitoring vital signs, tracking meals, reminding residents to take medication, and offering social interaction. ROSE helps reduce the workload of staff and improves the consistency of care delivery.

Design Tools

  • Scenario-Based Tool: Mind Map → Storyboard → Scratch prototype
  • Expression-Based Tool: Uses color + sound to communicate clearly, including for users with impairments
  • Embodiment-Based Tool: Card game for designing physical robot appearance
  • Behavior-Based Tool: Behavior Tree with Context Swap for failure-testing
  • Ethics-Based Tool: Mini Ethics Canvas for team-wide ethical consideration

Reflections

Design Research & HRI

Social robot design deeply aligns with HRI principles — scenario-building, embodiment and behavior mapping directly enhance interactive experiences.

Digital (AI) vs Embodiment

Embodiment strengthens emotional connection, trust, and perceived intelligence—more than voice-only assistants can achieve.

Why is 'HER' (not) a Compelling Argument?

While ‘HER’ shows emotional bonds with AI, it lacks the complexities of physical interaction and social context inherent in HRI.

Robot Revolution — When?

We are in a slow, silent revolution — care robots, warehouse bots, and home assistants are already shaping new norms.

Dinosaur Hotel (Henn-na Hotel)

The Japanese robot hotel failed because robots couldn't adapt to real-time guest needs — showing limits of static automation.

Nabaztag

Although simple, this WiFi rabbit robot pioneered ambient communication. Its failure shows the need for deeper usefulness and interaction.

CuddleBits

Minimal, emotional robots are powerful — showing how simplicity can outperform complexity when designed well.

Be Right Back..

Social robots cannot fully substitute human presence, but can bridge emotional and functional gaps in short spans.
